Combat


When one group engages another group; combat begins.

Combat between nations or between the UN and a nation, requires a formal declaration
of war.

Declarations Of War

Unless a unit is controlled by pirates or outlaws, all units in game are considered peaceful towards
each other until the outbreak of war. In order for a nation's units to attack each other, a declaration of
war must be declared.

In order to declare war, a Head of State, need only inform control that they are declaring war on another nation
Only the Head Of State can declare war on another nation. The declaration of war has consequences for the
Heads of State for both nations.

For each turn that a nation is at war, the Head of State for that nation will loose 2 credits
of income. For the UN, being at war with a nation means that the UN representative for that nation
looses their income until peace is restored.

Making Peace

Two parties my end a war at any time provided both parties agree to peace. Any reasonable demands may
be made and accepted to garner peace. Once peace is made between two parties, no declarations of war
may be made between parties for the rest of the game

Combat

When one fleet engages another fleet. Both player reveal the true size of their fleets.

Structures have a base initiative of 1 and ships in orbit have a base initiative of 3.

When combat is initiated, the first thing that occurs is that the initiative of the attacker
is compared to the initiative of the defender.

  1. If the attacker’s initiative is lower than the defenders, combat will only occur if
    the defender allows it.

  2. If the initiatives are equal, regular combat begins.

  3. If the attacker has a higher initiative to the defender, the attacker may either make
    a free attack, start a regular combat or retreat. Making a free attack lowers the attacker’s
    initiative by one. If lowering the attacker’s initiative makes the attacker’s initiative equal
    to an incoming fleet, the incoming fleet may initiate a regular combat. If after a free attack,
    the attacker still has a higher initiative, repeat cycle.

    Essentially an attacker with a higher initiative may use that advantage to make free attacks
    or ensure the defender cannot retreat. If they have 2 or more points of initiative they may do both.

    During a free attack, only the attacker fires.

    If there are incoming ships, and regular combat has begun, the incoming fleet enters combat on a round
    equal to the attacker’s initiative minus the incoming fleet’s initiative.

    Regular Combat

    During regular combat, if one side has a lower initiative or is a structure, that side my not retreat from battle.
    The defender fires first by picking a target and then rolling a d10. If the roll is higher than the CV
    of the target, the target takes a hit point of damage. If the attacking unit has more than one attack, the defender may
    do this a number of times equal to the number of attacks. The defender does this unit all of their units have fired.
    Any destroyed attackers are removed from the board and then the attacker fires and does the same as the defender.
    Repeat this unit one side retreats or one side is defeated.
